1. Agreeing to these terms

By creating an account or using STROKE you agree to these terms. If you are using STROKE on behalf of a club or squad, you confirm you are authorised to accept these terms for that organisation. If you do not agree, do not use the service.

These terms are governed by New Zealand law, and the New Zealand courts have non-exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute.

2. Your account

  • You must give accurate account information and keep your password secure. Anything done through your account is treated as done by you.
  • One account per person. Do not share logins — a shared coach login makes the audit log meaningless and exposes swimmer data to people who should not have it.
  • If you are under 16, you should have a parent or guardian's permission to hold an account. Where a squad requires guardian consent, the account is not activated until that consent is given.
  • Tell us promptly at hello@stroke.app if you think someone else has accessed your account.

3. Extra responsibilities for coaches and parents

A coach account holds other people's information, including information about children. If you use one, you accept that:

  • You will only add a swimmer to a squad where you have a legitimate coaching relationship with them, and the right to hold their information.
  • You are responsible for obtaining any consent your club, your national body or the law requires before entering a minor's details, and for using the consent flow where your squad requires it.
  • Notes you write about a swimmer are working coaching notes, not private diary entries: write them as though the swimmer or their guardian could one day lawfully request them, because under the Privacy Act 2020 they can.
  • You will only send a parent link to a person entitled to see that swimmer's information, and you will revoke it when that stops being true.
  • You will not use squad data for anything outside coaching that squad — no recruiting lists, no marketing, no passing it to a third party.

A parent account is read-only and limited to the swimmers it has been linked to. Attempting to reach any other swimmer's data is a breach of these terms.

4. Acceptable use

You must not:

  • Try to access data belonging to a swimmer, squad or club you are not entitled to see, including by guessing or altering identifiers in a URL or request.
  • Probe, scan, load-test or attempt to breach the service, or interfere with anyone else's use of it, without our written permission. Genuine security research is welcome — see the reporting section instead of doing it silently.
  • Scrape, bulk-export or systematically harvest data from the service, or use automated tools against it beyond normal use of the app.
  • Upload anything unlawful, abusive, or that infringes someone else's rights, or upload a photograph containing personal information you have no right to enter.
  • Resell, sublicense or white-label the service without our agreement.
  • Use the AI features to generate content that harasses or defames anyone.

We may suspend or terminate an account that breaches this section, and where the breach involves other people's data we may be legally required to report it.

The training data, sets, notes and images you put into STROKE remain yours. You give us the limited licence needed to operate the service: to store that content, process it, display it to the people entitled to see it, and send the necessary parts to the providers listed in the privacy policy so the features work.

We do not claim ownership of your training data, we do not sell it, and we do not use it to advertise to anyone.

6. AI-generated output

AI features in STROKE — squad summaries, generated training plans, the results-board scanner, the nutrition and dryland coach — produce suggestions, not instructions. They can be wrong.

Nothing STROKE generates is medical, physiotherapy, dietetic or safeguarding advice. A coach remains fully responsible for what they ask their swimmers to do. A swimmer remains responsible for their own health decisions. If something hurts, see a professional, not a chatbot.

The results-board scanner requires you to confirm the times it reads before anything is saved, because handwriting recognition on a wet whiteboard is imperfect. You are responsible for what you confirm.

You can stop using STROKE at any time and ask us to delete your account by emailing privacy@stroke.app. We may suspend or end an account that breaches these terms, or where we are required to by law. If a coach's account ends, the swimmers in that squad keep their own training records — those belong to the swimmer.
