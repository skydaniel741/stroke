1. Who we are

STROKE ("we", "us") is a swim squad and training platform operated from New Zealand. This policy explains what personal information we collect, why we collect it, who can see it, and what you can do about it. It applies to every account type: coach, swimmer (free and Solo) and parent.

Because we operate from New Zealand, we handle personal information in line with the Privacy Act 2020 and its thirteen information privacy principles. Where a user is in the United Kingdom or the European Economic Area, we also handle their personal data consistently with the UK GDPR / EU GDPR, including the rights set out in section 10 below.

2. What we collect

We collect information in three ways: what you give us when you create an account and use the app, what your coach or your swimmer enters about you, and a small amount of technical information needed to keep you signed in and the service running.

CategoryWhat it isWhy we need it
Account details Username, email address, role (coach, swimmer or parent), and your password stored only as a bcrypt hash To create and secure your account and let you sign in
Social sign-in If you sign in with Google or Apple, the email address and display name that provider gives us To authenticate you without a separate password
Training data Swim times, events, course (25m/50m), splits, sessions, sets, personal bests, goals, training notes and check-ins you enter This is the product: it is what your log, analytics and plans are built from
Squad data Club and squad names, roster membership, lane or training group, attendance records, calendar entries To run a squad: rosters, attendance, scheduling
Coach-entered data A coach's private notes about a swimmer, and injury or availability status flags To let a coach keep working notes on their own squad
Parent link data A parent's name and email address, and the record of which swimmer they are linked to To give a parent read-only access to their own swimmer
Guardian consent Where a squad requires parental consent for a minor, the guardian's name, email and the fact consent was given To show that consent was obtained before a minor's account was activated
Uploaded images Photographs of a results board or whiteboard that a coach uploads for the scanner To read the times off the board and file them against the right swimmers
Technical data A session cookie, a "remember me" cookie if you choose it, server logs, and failed-login counts used for lockout To keep you signed in, keep the service running and defend against brute-force attacks

We do not collect payment card details ourselves. We do not collect location data, contacts, or anything from your device beyond what a normal website receives.

3. Why we use it

  • To run your account — signing you in, saving your swims, showing your history, personal bests and points.
  • To run features you or your coach use — attendance, sets, squad analytics, generated training plans, the results-board scanner, the parent view, printed reports.
  • To send account email — verification codes, password-related email, and, if you are in a squad, your coach's announcements.
  • To keep the service secure — detecting abuse, rate-limiting sign-in attempts, keeping an admin audit log of administrative actions.

We do not use your information for advertising, we do not sell it, and we do not share it with data brokers. There is no advertising in STROKE.

5. Who we share it with outside STROKE

We use a small number of service providers, each for one job:

  • Our hosting and database provider — to run the application and store its data.
  • Resend — to deliver account email such as verification codes.
  • Anthropic — to power the AI features described in section 6, when you or your coach use one.
  • Google / Apple — only if you choose to sign in with one of them, and only to authenticate you.

Some of these providers process data outside New Zealand. Where personal information is sent overseas, we only use providers who are required to protect it to a standard comparable to the Privacy Act 2020, as required by information privacy principle 12.

We will also disclose information if we are legally required to, or where it is necessary to prevent a serious threat to someone's safety. We will not disclose it for any other reason, and we do not sell it to anyone.

6. AI features

Several features use a large language model provided by Anthropic: the squad and swimmer summaries, the training plan generator, the results-board scanner, the nutrition and dryland coach chat, and the coach-reviewed parent digest.

  • Only the data needed for that request is sent — for example, the times and sessions being summarised, or the photo of the results board.
  • The provider processes it to answer that request. Your data is not used to train the provider's models.
  • AI output is a starting point, not a verdict. Training, nutrition and dryland guidance from STROKE is general information and is not medical, physiotherapy or dietetic advice.
  • A parent digest is generated as a draft and is only visible to a parent after a coach has reviewed and approved it.

7. Children and young swimmers

Swimming is a sport with a lot of young athletes, so this matters more here than in most software.

  • Where a squad requires it, a coach can mark a squad invite as needing guardian consent. In that case the swimmer's account is not activated until a parent or guardian confirms consent and provides their name and email.
  • A parent or guardian can ask us at any time to show them what we hold about their child, correct it, or delete it, and we will act on that promptly.
  • A young swimmer's data is never made public by default. The leaderboard requires an explicit opt-in.
  • If you believe we hold information about a child that should not be there, contact us using section 11 and we will remove it.

8. How long we keep it

  • While your account is active — we keep your account and training data so your history is there season after season.
  • If you delete your account — we delete your personal information, other than a minimal record we are required to keep for security or legal reasons, such as an entry in the administrative audit log.
  • Results-board photographs — kept only as long as needed to process and confirm the times read from them.
  • Server logs — kept for a short period for security and debugging, then discarded.

If you leave a squad, your own training record stays with your account. It belongs to you, not to the club.

9. How we protect it

The full detail is on the data safety page. In summary: passwords are hashed with bcrypt and never stored in readable form; access is scoped by account role and checked on the server for every request, not just hidden in the interface; sign-in attempts are rate-limited and accounts lock temporarily after repeated failures; and traffic is encrypted in transit. No system is perfectly secure, but we take reasonable steps, and if a privacy breach occurs that is likely to cause serious harm we will notify the Office of the Privacy Commissioner and affected people, as the Privacy Act 2020 requires.

10. Your rights

Under the Privacy Act 2020 you have the right to access the personal information we hold about you and to ask us to correct it if it is wrong. Where the GDPR applies to you, you also have rights to erasure, restriction, portability, and to object to certain processing.

In practice, you can ask us to:

  • Tell you what we hold about you, and give you a copy
  • Correct anything inaccurate
  • Delete your account and the personal information in it
  • Revoke a parent's access to a swimmer (you can also do this yourself, immediately, from the app)

Email privacy@stroke.app. We will respond within 20 working days, which is the timeframe the Privacy Act sets. There is no charge for a reasonable request.

If you are not satisfied with how we have handled a privacy issue, you can complain to the New Zealand Office of the Privacy Commissioner at privacy.org.nz. If the GDPR applies to you, you may complain to your local supervisory authority.

11. Changes to this policy

If we make a material change, we will update the date at the top of this page and, where the change matters to you, tell you in the app or by email. Continuing to use STROKE after a change means the updated policy applies.

12. Contact

Privacy questions, access requests and complaints: privacy@stroke.app.

Anything else: hello@stroke.app.
