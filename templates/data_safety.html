How we keep your data safe.

A swim squad's records include children's names, times, attendance and a coach's candid notes. That deserves a page that says specifically what we do, not a paragraph saying we take security seriously.

The rule everything else follows from

Hiding a link is not security. Every boundary described on this page is enforced when the server builds the response, not by leaving a button off a page. If a parent account asks the server for another swimmer's data by editing the address bar, the server refuses — it does not simply fail to link there.

Passwords and sign-in

  • Passwords are hashed with bcrypt before storage. We never store, log or transmit your plain password, and nobody at STROKE can look it up or tell you what it is.
  • Repeated failed sign-ins lock an account temporarily, so an attacker cannot sit there guessing passwords.
  • New accounts must verify their email address with a code before they can be used, which stops someone registering an account under an address they do not own.
  • If you sign in with Google or Apple, we never see a password at all.
  • Traffic between your browser and STROKE is encrypted in transit over HTTPS. Session cookies are marked HTTP-only and same-site, so page scripts cannot read them and other sites cannot ride on them.
  • The session signing key is required at startup — the app refuses to boot in production with a guessable one rather than running insecurely.

The access boundaries, precisely

STROKE has four kinds of account and each one is scoped deliberately.

Swimmers

A swimmer sees their own training data and nothing belonging to anyone else. The only exception is the leaderboard, which is off by default and shows names and times only among swimmers who have each explicitly opted in.

Coaches

A coach sees the swimmers in their own squads. Actions on a swimmer are checked against squad membership on the server, not just against the squad in the URL: a request to attach a note or a status flag to someone who is not a member of that squad is rejected, even if that squad belongs to the coach making the request. That specific hole is covered by an automated regression test so it cannot quietly come back.

A coach at another club sees nothing of yours.

Parents

A parent account can only reach swimmers it has been explicitly linked to, through a link generated by that swimmer or their coach. The dashboard resolves which swimmer to show from the set of links belonging to that parent, so asking for a different swimmer's identifier does not widen access — it falls back to a swimmer they are already entitled to see.

The parent view deliberately excludes two things the coach can see: private coach notes and injury or availability flags. Those exist so a coach can keep candid working notes; a parent view that leaked them would quietly end that candour, which would be worse for the swimmer. A parent link can be revoked at any time and access ends immediately.

Administrators

Administrative access exists to operate, support and secure the service. Administrative actions are written to an audit log, so who did what is recoverable rather than a matter of trust.

Data sent to AI features

  • Only what the request needs is sent — the times being summarised, the photo of the results board, the sessions being planned around.
  • The provider processes it to answer that request. Your data is not used to train their models.
  • The results-board scanner never writes anything to a swimmer's record until a coach has reviewed and confirmed the rows it read.
  • A parent digest is generated as a draft and stays invisible to the parent until a coach approves it. A draft is not a published document.

Young swimmers

  • Nothing about a swimmer is public by default. The leaderboard requires an explicit opt-in.
  • Where a squad requires guardian consent, a minor's account is not activated until a guardian confirms it, and that consent is recorded.
  • A parent or guardian can ask for access to, correction of, or deletion of their child's information at any time.

Operational practice

  • Uploads are size-capped, so a huge file gets a clean rejection rather than tying up the service.
  • Input is validated before it reaches the database. If an error still occurs, the transaction is rolled back so a half-written record cannot corrupt your history, and you get a plain error page rather than a stack trace exposing internals.
  • External URLs used by the research importer are validated to prevent the server being pointed at internal addresses.
  • The access-control rules described above are covered by an automated test suite that runs against a throwaway database, including explicit regression tests for previously-found access bugs.

What we are still building

Being specific means also being specific about the gaps. STROKE is an early-stage product built by a small team, and it would be dishonest to present it as a hardened enterprise platform.

  • We have not yet had an independent third-party security audit or penetration test.
  • We do not currently offer two-factor authentication. It is on the list.
  • We do not hold a formal certification such as ISO 27001 or SOC 2.

If any of that is a blocker for your club, tell us — it helps us prioritise.

If something goes wrong

If a privacy breach occurs that is likely to cause serious harm, the Privacy Act 2020 requires us to notify the Office of the Privacy Commissioner and the people affected, and we will. We will tell you what happened, what was involved and what to do, without waiting to have a tidy story ready first.

Reporting a vulnerability

If you have found a security problem, please tell us at security@stroke.app before telling anyone else, and give us a reasonable chance to fix it. We will not pursue anyone who reports a genuine issue in good faith and does not access, alter or retain other people's data while investigating.

Please do not run automated scanners or load tests against the live service — there are real squads' sessions on the other side of it.

Getting your data, or deleting it

Email privacy@stroke.app and ask us to show you what we hold, correct it, export it, or delete it. We respond within 20 working days, the timeframe set by the Privacy Act 2020. Personal bests can also be exported from inside the app at any time.
